## 6. Industry Analysis

### Porter's Five Forces Assessment

#### 1. Threat of New Entrants: **MEDIUM-HIGH**

**Factors Increasing Threat:**
- Low capital requirements for basic AI agent platforms
- Open-source LLMs (Llama, Mistral) reduce AI barriers
- Big tech (AWS, Google, Microsoft) have resources to enter quickly
- No regulatory barriers to entry

**Factors Decreasing Threat:**
- Separation of powers architecture is complex to replicate
- Network effects favor established marketplace
- Trust Score history creates switching costs
- Compliance certification requires time to build credibility

**Assessment:** New entrants can build basic agent platforms easily, but replicating the governance + marketplace + trust trinity is harder. First-mover advantage matters.

#### 2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers: **LOW**

**Key Suppliers:**
- LLM providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) â€” multiple options, commoditizing
- Cloud infrastructure (AWS, GCP, Azure) â€” highly competitive, standardized
- Blockchain/hash chain infrastructure â€” open-source options available

**Assessment:** Supplier power is low. AgentAnchor can switch LLM providers, cloud vendors are competing on price, and core infrastructure is commoditized.

#### 3. Bargaining Power of Buyers: **MEDIUM**

**Enterprise Buyers:**
- High switching costs once Trust Score history accumulated
- Compliance requirements create lock-in (audit trail can't be moved)
- But large enterprises have negotiating leverage on pricing

**SMB/Consumer Buyers:**
- Lower switching costs
- Price-sensitive
- But value trust signals highly

**Trainers (as buyers of platform access):**
- Can multi-home across platforms initially
- But audience and Trust Score create loyalty over time

**Assessment:** Buyer power is moderate. Enterprise lock-in through compliance data offsets negotiating leverage.

#### 4. Threat of Substitutes: **MEDIUM**

**Direct Substitutes:**
- In-house AI governance (build vs buy) â€” costly but possible for large enterprises
- Consulting firms offering manual AI audits â€” doesn't scale
- Traditional GRC platforms adding AI features â€” bolt-on, not native

**Indirect Substitutes:**
- "Trust but verify" approaches (just hope AI doesn't fail)
- Avoiding AI agents altogether (but this is declining)

**Assessment:** In-house builds are the main substitute threat, but the complexity and regulatory expertise required favor specialized platforms.

#### 5. Competitive Rivalry: **LOW-MEDIUM** (Currently)

**Current State:**
- No direct competitor with governance + marketplace + trust + audit trail
- AI governance startups are compliance-focused (no marketplace)
- AI agent platforms are execution-focused (no governance)
- Market is fragmented and nascent

**Future Projection:**
- Rivalry will intensify as market matures
- Big tech entry is likely within 2-3 years
- First-mover with network effects wins

**Assessment:** Competitive rivalry is currently LOW because the category doesn't exist yet. This is the window of opportunity. Will increase to HIGH within 3-5 years.

### Overall Industry Attractiveness: **HIGH**

The combination of low supplier power, growing buyer needs (governance gap), nascent competition, and regulatory tailwinds makes this a highly attractive market. The key is moving fast to establish category leadership before big tech or well-funded startups enter.
